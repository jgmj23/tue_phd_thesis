## Required Thesis Components

- Dissertation may be written in Dutch or English.
- An English title and English summary are required.
- A short curriculum vitae is required.
- Rector-approved title page is required.
- The dissertation must be printed.
- The dissertation should not include advertisements.
- Acknowledgements may be included if suitably discreet and discussed with the first promotor.
- If the dissertation consists of multiple scientific treatises, they must cohere around a topic and include a summary chapter.

## Process Constraints

- Draft dissertation must be uploaded in Hora Finita no later than 3 months before the defense.
- Committee feedback/approval and Dean approval should be complete at least 6 weeks before the defense.
- The recto and verso title page must be approved by the Rector before printing.
- After Rector approval, no changes may be made to the approved title-page content.
- The final PDF must be supplied to the university/library process at least 4 weeks before the defense.
- Printed dissertations and propositions, if any, must be sent to the chair and Doctorate Committee members at least 2 weeks before the defense.
- If distribution requirements are missed, the defense date may be postponed.

## Public Summary

- A public summary is part of TU/e PhD completion.
- Maximum length: 600 words.
- It is submitted via Hora Finita after the dissertation is submitted to the Doctorate Committee.
- It is not included in the dissertation.
- It may be published on the TU/e website and used for communication with journalists.

## Propositions

Propositions are optional. If included:

- They must be approved by the first promotor as defensible.
- They must be submitted for Rector approval at least 6 weeks before the defense.
- At least six propositions must not relate to the dissertation topic.
- Of those unrelated propositions, four must be scientific/technical and two must be generally social.
- Propositions should include references or sources where possible.
- Propositions must express the candidate's own opinion, not merely quotations.

## Title Page Rules

The title page should follow the TU/e model title page.

- Use `PROEFSCHRIFT` for a dissertation.
- Defense date should be written in full in Dutch and not in capitals.
- Candidate name must match passport name exactly.
- For a birth outside the Netherlands, place and country of birth must be listed according to passport and noted in Dutch.
- Affiliations from Dutch universities must be written in Dutch.
- Only committee members from outside TU/e get affiliations after their names.
- TU/e-affiliated committee members get no affiliation after their names.
- Current titles should be used: `dr.`, `dr.ir.`, `prof.dr.`, `prof.dr.ir.`, etc.
- Printing, cover layout, ISBN numbers, and cooperative-organization logos must not be written on the title page. If needed, add that information on a separate dissertation page.
- The required scientific-integrity statement appears on the reverse/title-page block.
